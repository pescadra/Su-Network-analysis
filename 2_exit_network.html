ANALYSIS 5: Connectivity vs Density Paradox¶

Why each investor gets better connected while the ecosystem feels "looser"¶

What is degree?

Degree = the average number of other investors that one investor co-invests with. If your degree is 8, you have shared a deal with 8 different partners. Higher = more connected.

What is density?

Density = the fraction of all possible partnerships that actually exist. If there are 100 investors, there are 4,950 possible pairs. If 500 of those pairs have actually co-invested, density = 500/4,950 = 10%. Higher = everyone knows everyone.

The paradox in plain language:

Imagine a networking event.

  • In 2014 there were ~20 people in the room. Each person knew about 2 others, and together they covered ~13% of all possible introductions.
  • By 2026 the room has over 700 people. Each person now knows about 11 others (5× better connected!) — but 11 out of 700 is less than 2% of the room.

So what happened?

  • Each investor now co-invests with more partners than ever (degree: ~2 → 11.1)
  • But the number of people in the room grew so fast that the percentage of the room you know dropped (density: ~13% → 1.6%)
  • The room grew ~38× (~20 → 700+), possible handshakes grew ~1,500×, but actual handshakes grew ~180×

Bottom line: Every investor is better connected than before. The falling density is just a mathematical side-effect of rapid growth — not a sign of fragmentation. This is the normal pattern in every healthy growing network (Barabási, 2002).
